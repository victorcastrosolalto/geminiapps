The Core of Computing

A computer is a machine that executes instructions to perform specific tasks, operating on a model of input, processing, and output. These devices are ubiquitous, from embedded systems in appliances to personal computers and the large-scale mainframes and servers powering our infrastructure.

Hardware & Software

The architecture of modern computers consists of two primary elements: physical hardware (CPU, RAM, storage) and instructional software (Operating Systems, applications). This synergy enables all computing functions.
